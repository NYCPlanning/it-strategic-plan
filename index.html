Vision & Values

Our vision is to be a world class IT service delivery organization centered on six core values...

Customer-centricity

Designing and delivering IT solutions that put the needs of clients first

Efficiency

Delivering technology solutions in a cost-effective, efficient and transparent manner

Innovation

Fostering a culture of innovation and adoption of new technologies

Quality

Maintaining the standards of high quality data, systems and information

Sustainability

Embracing best-practices in delivering sustainable and secure technology platforms

Collaboration

Working collaboratively with our partners on providing technology platforms of the highest value

Strategic Objectives

Our IT strategic objectives are aligned with the agency's strategic objectives

Optimize IT Service Delivery

Provide reliable and cost-effective modern IT services

  • Maintain open channels with customers to continuously improve core services and infrastructure
  • Improve internal communication of DCP technology landscape, including ongoing upgrades

Short-term Initiatives

Maintain open channels with customers to continuously improve core services and infrastructure
  • Better WiFi Coverage at 120 Broadway
  • Redesign POPS Database
  • Establish Technology Steering Committee
  • Improve file sharing with other agencies and clients
  • Develop Application & Infrastructure Modernization Roadmap
  • Extend ticketing system to Facilities and Human Capital
  • Expand internet bandwidth to support increasing demand by users and cloud based applications and storage
  • Video Conferencing for HQ Meetings
  • Adopt ITIL methodologies for release management, configuration management and unified monitoring
  • IT Inventory System and Decommission legacy PCNS Inventory
  • Implement IT and Telecom infrastructure into new Bronx office space
  • Annual check in with end users and divisions for hardware/software needs
  • Continuously evaluate and implement leading edge security measure to protect infrastructure and comply with citywide standards and best-practices
  • Establish software metering to track and maintain software licenses in use
  • Adopt methodology for evaluating new software to meet agency needs/requests
Improve internal communication of DCP technology landscape, including ongoing upgrades
  • Develop and implement internal communications protocol
  • Support reorganization of DCP Commons, especially in areas of planning guides
  • Reorganize DCP Commons to include information about technology services and product offerings

Leverage Technology for Agency Effectiveness

Ensure technological solutions are considered as part of all agency processes

  • Support DCP as planning leader by identifying and adopting technology innovations aligned with agency priorities
  • Introduce and support automated workflow systems for more efficient process management and better decision making
  • Enhance modeling and analytics capabilities to achieve better planning insights

Short-term Initiatives

Support DCP as planning leader by identifying and adopting technology innovations aligned with agency priorities
  • Establish NYC Planning Labs to research and develop innovative planning platforms for analysis and decision making in partnership with DCP planning divisions
  • Investigate expanded use of cloud for hosting, collaboration, data processing
  • Establish best practices for document sharing and management (Sharepoint)
  • Expand GIS Infrastructure Offerings Beyond Esri
Introduce and support automated workflow systems for more efficient process management and better decision making
  • Develop and Deploy Paperless Filing for Land Use Applications
  • Re-engineer CD Needs and Budget Request Technology & Reporting Platform
Enhance modeling and analytics capabilities to achieve better planning insights
  • Revaluate, recommend and implement Agency's 3D Modeling Software Needs
  • Enhance Soft Site Tool
  • Develop tool for generating tax maps for large area proposals

Excel at Planning Information & Data Stewardship

Enrich data resources, practices, and policy to align with planning and citywide objectives

  • Expand leadership role in geospatial technologies and locational information
  • Lead application of best practices in database development and management
  • Prioritize data partnerships to support citywide and regional planning

Short-term Initiatives

Expand leadership role in geospatial technologies and locational information
  • Enhance Geosupport access application to include online public facing batch processing
  • Implement agency-wide roll-out of Cyclomedia high-resolution street imagery
  • Establish agency best practices for geocoding
  • Replatform ZoLa with additional data and features
  • Update Geosupport Property Address Database (PAD) maintenance system
  • Enhance Geosupport access application to include web services
  • Develop neighborhood profile
  • Develop self-service mapping
Maintain leadership in database development and management
  • Provide IT Support for Census 2020 LUCA (Local Update of Census Addresses) Review
  • Enhance Census FactFinder
  • Establish central data management group to set standards, best practices, manage catalog, data stewards & diplomacy
  • Promote data awareness in the agency by creating well-maintained data catalog
  • Establish best practices to support consistent data development and management with emphasis on high quality, maintainability and sustainability
  • Identify appropriate maintenance strategy for data critical to planning functions
  • Support federated model of data stewardship
  • Streamline production of strategic data products
Prioritize data partnerships to support citywide and regional planning
  • Establish legal frameworks and MOUs for data sharing with city agencies and regional entities

Support Public Transparency & Engagement

Enable more active, open, and inclusive community engagement through technology

  • Develop and support platforms to increase data accessibility and planning transparency
  • Facilitate inclusive citizen engagement and outreach methods

Short-term Initiatives

Develop and support platforms to increase data accessibility and planning transparency
  • Enhance accessibility and functionality of online Zoning Resolution including annotation
Facilitate inclusive citizen engagement and outreach methods
  • Support community engagement platform for projects, environmental review and land use process
  • Address hearing Room/CPC Tech Needs
  • Electronic sign-in for CPC meetings

Invest in Human Capital

Foster culture of IT talent development, high performance & diversity

  • Provide training and professional development opportunities for all IT staff
  • Identify and address gaps in current staffing and IT roles
  • Promote opportunities for career advancement in and outside of ITD
  • Establish IT culture that promotes work-life balance

Short-term Initiatives

Provide training and professional development opportunities for all IT staff
  • Foster IT inter-sectional project teams (possibly with participation of tech staff across DCP) to encourage skill sharing and development
  • Identify and engage enterprise-wide, online training provider (e.g., Lynda.com) for all tech staff
  • Schedule division-wide meetings three times a year
  • Communicate technology priorities, plans, needs and opportunities with IT staff
  • Continuously right-size IT training budgets and identify new areas for professional development
  • Establish and enforce allocated time for training appropriate for skill development of IT staff
  • Provide opportunities for IT staff to present their work to their division colleagues
Identify and address gaps in current staffing and IT roles
  • Expand internal software development capabilities
  • Expand ability to handle multiple projects
  • Enhance UI/UX skills in ITD
  • Stronger planning representation in ITD
  • Investigate 3D Animation Capability within DCP
